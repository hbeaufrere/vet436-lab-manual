Occlusal Adjustments

Occlusal Adjustments of Incisors, Premolars and Molars

Premolars and Molars

Instruments

  • Straight handpiece (low-speed)
  • Medium round diamond bur
  • Cement spatula
  • Tongue spatula
  • Q-tips

Procedure

Using the endoscope for visualization, perform occlusal adjustment of the premolars and molars (cheek teeth):

  1. Place the round diamond bur on the straight handpiece (low-speed).
  2. Under endoscopic guidance, identify the cheek teeth that require adjustment (e.g., spurs, uneven occlusal plane, elongated crowns).
  3. Use a cement spatula to protect the soft tissues (buccal mucosa and tongue) from the rotating bur.
  4. Apply the rotating diamond bur to the occlusal surface of the cheek teeth with a light touch. The goal is to restore a normal, flat occlusal plane.
  5. Work progressively, removing small amounts of tooth at a time. Frequently reassess the occlusal plane with the endoscope.
  6. Avoid thermal damage: use a light, intermittent touch rather than continuous pressure. No cooling fluid is used, so brief contact is essential to prevent overheating the tooth and damaging the pulp.
  7. Use Q-tips to remove debris from the oral cavity during the procedure.

Perform the Adjustment

Perform occlusal adjustment of the cheek teeth on your specimen. Restore a normal occlusal plane.

Incisors

Instruments

  • High-speed angled handpiece
  • Cylindrical diamond bur
  • Tongue depressor

Procedure

Perform crown reduction of the incisors:

  1. Remove the pouch dilator to access the incisors.
  2. Place a tongue depressor between the upper and lower incisors to protect the opposing teeth and soft tissues during the procedure.
  3. Attach the cylindrical diamond bur to the high-speed angled handpiece.
  4. Reduce the crown height of the incisor to restore the normal chisel-shaped occlusal plane.
  5. Avoid exposing the pulp cavity — leave at least 0.5 mm coronal to the gingival papilla to maintain an adequate safety margin.
  6. Shape the incisor to restore the proper chisel shape and angle of the occlusal surface.

Perform the Adjustment

Perform incisor crown reduction on your specimen. After the adjustment, clean the oral cavity with Q-tips and remove any debris.

Check Occlusion

After completing all adjustments, check both premolar-molar occlusion and incisor occlusion:

  • The cheek teeth occlusal plane should be flat and even.
  • The mandibular incisors should rest between the maxillary peg teeth (I3) and the main upper incisors (I1).
  • The chisel edge of the mandibular incisor should contact the palatal surface of the maxillary I1.
